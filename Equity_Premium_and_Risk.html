Equity Premium and Risk

This notebook implements in python the lecture by Prof. John Cochrane available on Youtube. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rgC5Sz9pjZE&list=PLAXSVuGaw0KxTEN_cy-RCuEzzRdnF_xtx&index=6. In this lecture the focus is on facts about the stock market.

Fact 1: Stocks pay more (much more) than bonds

In the graph below we are going to show the real (inflation adjusted) value of 1$ invested in the SP500 in 1985 vs the same dollor invested in bonds (proxied by the 3-month t-bill).

In[19]:
fig, ax = plt.subplots(figsize=(10, 6))
ax.plot(df['year'], df['Investment_Value_Stock'], label = 'Stocks', color='blue', marker='o')
ax.plot(df['year'], df['Investment_Value_Risk_Free'], label = 'T-Bills', color = 'red', marker = 's')
#add a horizontal line at y=1
ax.axhline(y=1, color='gray', linestyle='--', alpha= 0.5)
ax.set_xlabel('Year')
#show the year only every 5 years
ax.xaxis.set_major_locator(plt.MaxNLocator(10))
ax.set_ylabel('Investment Value')
ax.set_title('Real Value of $1 Invested in 1985')
ax.legend()
plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()

Fact 2: Stocks are more volatile than bonds

The economic explenation as to why stocks pay more than bonds, is that stocks must be riskier. A way of visualizing this risk is to plot annual returns.

In[23]:
